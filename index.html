RoA-Planner toward Multi-Layered Spaces

Yeongwoo Son1
Sungkyunkwan University1

Abstract

While the conventional 3D RoA-Planner [1] efficiently generates collision-free SE(3) paths for quadruped robots via 2D search, it is limited to environments represented by a single height map, making it inapplicable to multi-layered or multi-floor structures. To address this limitation, we propose an enhanced framework that integrates the methodology of the PCT-Planner [2] for multi-layer navigation. In PCT-Planner, by decomposing a complex 3D environment into multiple layers and generating ground and ceiling tomograms for each, it is possible to transform the computationally intensive 3D path planning problem into a series of efficient 2D tasks. In this work, leveraging these tomograms, we generate 3D Rotatable Areas (RoAs) that accounts for multi-floor constraints and represent the entire workspace as a Multi-Layered RoA-Map. Optimal paths are then computed using the A* algorithm. This approach successfully extends the 3D RoA-Planner to multi-layered environments, significantly enhancing the robot's mobility in complex, real-world scenarios.

Methodologies and Results

Rotatable Area

In this study, the Rotatable Area (RoA) is extended from the conventional 3D RoA to incorporate multi-floor environmental data.

$$ R = (l, g_u, g_d, \mathbf{p}, \mathbf{R}, \psi_s, \psi_e, \tau) \tag{1} $$

The standard 3D RoA represents the collision-free yaw range $[\psi_s, \psi_e]$ at a given position $\mathbf{p}$, where the robot’s collision model is aligned with the roll and pitch of the terrain normal. It also includes $\tau$, which denotes the traversability of the area.

To enable path planning in multi-layered structures, we introduce additional state variables to the existing RoA framework:

  • $l$: The specific layer index where the RoA is generated.
  • $g_u$ / $g_d$: Indicators for upward or downward connectivity, representing whether the RoA serves as a transition point to an adjacent layer.

By integrating these parameters, the proposed framework effectively represents 3D connectivity, allowing for robust global planning across multiple floors and complex vertical structures.

Mapping

The Rotatable Area (RoA), denoted by $R$, is defined as the continuous range of yaw heading angles within which the robot can rotate at position $(x, y)$ without colliding with obstacles.

The parameters $\psi_s, \psi_e \in [0, 2\pi]$ denote the start and end of the RoA, and all parameters are referenced to the world frame. If the robot’s heading angle is between $\psi_s$ and $\psi_e$ at $(x, y)$, the robot remains collision-free. Within the range corresponding to RoA, the robot can rotate freely.

Planning

Our approach regards local motion as a relationship between two RoAs, $R_1 \rightarrow R_2$. Local motion consists of 1) performing rotation at $R_1$ from the initial heading angle $\theta_1$ to the target heading angle $\theta_2$, and 2) then translation to the target position of $R_2$. The feasiblility of local motion is determined by area condition and edge condition. If local motion is feasible, a robot can safely traverse between two RoAs without collision.
